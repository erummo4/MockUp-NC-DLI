October 14-15, 2025

North Carolina Innovation Accelerator

The Umstead Hotel & Spa, Cary, NC

This is the Institute's signature launch convening, a two-day working experience for superintendents, selected cabinet leaders, and board members ready to engage seriously with the forces reshaping public education. Participants will examine what is changing in education, reflect on what North Carolina's 2030 priorities mean in their specific local context, and honestly assess their district's current conditions for change.

This is not a sit-and-listen event. Leaders come ready to work, and leave with a clearer diagnosis of where their system is ready to move and where it is stuck. By the end of the two days, participants will walk away with a sharper picture of their district's innovation readiness, practical frameworks for engaging their communities in redesign, and new relationships with a peer cohort of North Carolina leaders facing similar challenges and opportunities. Light receptions both evenings create time and space for the relationship-building and joy that makes the work sustainable.

Who can attend: Superintendents and up to 2 district/board leaders per district

January 22, 2026

AI and the Future of Learning: A Leadership Workshop

Location TBD, Raleigh, NC

This one-day convening is designed for superintendents and senior district leaders who want to move past the hype and get practical about artificial intelligence, both as a tool for student learning and a new reality that's reshaping the uniquely human skills our young people most need. Participants will explore how AI is already reshaping classrooms, workforce expectations, and community concerns, and will work through real scenarios and decision frameworks together.

The day is structured around active problem-solving with facilitators from both The Innovation Project and Transcend guiding teams through applied exercises. Participants will leave a clearer sense of their own vision for learning with AI, guidance for piloting new approaches and how to communicate about AI with boards and families, and new connections with peers across the state who are navigating the same terrain. Districts are encouraged, though not required, to bring a small team to maximize what they take home.

Who can attend: Superintendents, CAOs, chief innovation officers, and senior district leaders

March 4-5, 2026

Redesigning High School: A Hands-on Workshop for District Teams.

The Proximity Hotel, Greensboro, NC

High school is where urgency and complexity collide, and where the gap between what students need and what most systems currently offer is most pronounced. This two-day convening invites district teams to examine the assumptions built into the traditional high school model, learn from emerging redesign examples from across North Carolina and beyond, and begin mapping what a more relevant and future-ready high school experience could look like in their community.

Facilitated by Transcend's school design experts alongside North Carolina practitioners, sessions blend big-picture challenges with grounded, local application. Teams will leave with a richer understanding of what new models and innovations are thriving at the high school level, an initial diagnostic of their high school's strengths and constraints, and a set of actionable next steps. This convening is intentionally designed for teams given the value of collaboration and alignment among the superintendent, board leadership, and school-level leaders from the start. Dinner on the first evening is included and will feature a facilitated conversation with a North Carolina superintendent who is already leading this work.

Who can attend: District teams of 3-5 (superintendent + board and/or school leaders); limited to 12 teams statewide
